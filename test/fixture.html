The Weekender: Family Fun Ideas for Seattle, the Eastside and South Sound

My top 10 weekend events are not to be missed, either. Take a spin in a classic car and have a blast at a festival. See you out there!

Take a Spin at LeMay

Date: Friday, July 17
Cost: Included with admission ($17.50–$24.50) or membership
Location: LeMay – America’s Car Museum, 2702 E. D St., Tacoma

If you’ve ever visited LeMay, you’ve likely daydreamed about taking a spin in one of the cars from the museum’s collections and wandering the exhibits all afternoon.

Fircrest Fun Days

Date: Friday–Saturday, July 17–18
Cost: Free
Location: Fircrest Park, 555 Contra Costa Ave., Fircrest

Travel back in time this weekend to the Old West with this themed festival featuring live concerts, vendor booths, food trucks and fireworks on Saturday night.

Lavender Festival

Date: Saturday–Sunday, July 18–19
Cost: $9
Location: JB Family Growers, 14063 N.E. 145th St., Woodinville

While I’d love to make the trek out to Sequim, this lavender festival on the Eastside is closer to home and just as delightful, with fields of 15,000 plants in full bloom.

Parade of Nations and Watch Party

Date: Sunday, July 19
Cost: Free
Location: Rainier Playfields, 3700 S. Alaska St., Seattle

Celebrate before the final match with a parade that highlights diversity and unity across cultures, plus soccer-themed activities and food trucks for lunch.
